What I help with

## Patterns that therapy can change

CBT works by identifying the cycle keeping the problem going — then testing new ways of responding. These are some of the areas I work with regularly.

### Anxiety & overthinking

Racing thoughts that won't switch off. "What if" scenarios on loop. Physical tension you can't shake. We map the worry cycle and build practical ways to step out of it.

[How I work with anxiety →](https://mackjwellys.github.io/vision-wellbeing-demo/the-therapy.html#anxiety)

### Perfectionism

High standards that started as motivation have become the thing in the way. We build flexibility without lowering your standards.

[How I work with perfectionism →](https://mackjwellys.github.io/vision-wellbeing-demo/the-therapy.html#perfectionism)

### Health anxiety

Constant worry about symptoms. Googling conditions late at night. Seeking reassurance that never quite lasts. We break the checking cycle.

[How I work with health anxiety →](https://mackjwellys.github.io/vision-wellbeing-demo/the-therapy.html#health-anxiety)

### Low mood & burnout

Everything feels heavy and pointless. Activities that used to bring satisfaction now feel like obligations. We start small and let momentum build.

[How I work with low mood →](https://mackjwellys.github.io/vision-wellbeing-demo/the-therapy.html#mood)

### OCD, panic, insomnia, performance anxiety — and more

I also work with OCD-spectrum difficulties, panic and physical anxiety symptoms, insomnia, exam stress, social anxiety, phobias, self-esteem, and adjustment to life changes. The model adapts; the structure underneath doesn't.

[See all areas I work with →](https://mackjwellys.github.io/vision-wellbeing-demo/the-therapy.html)

How it works

## Three steps to getting started

1

### Free consultation

A 15-minute video call to talk through what's going on and whether CBT with me is the right fit. No obligation.

2

### Structured sessions

50-minute sessions via secure video. We map the pattern, test new responses, and build practical skills. Typically 8–16 sessions with a planned ending.

3

### Lasting change

The aim is not to stay in therapy. It's to leave with a clearer understanding of your patterns and the confidence to manage them on your own.

About Jack

## Research-informed therapy from someone who understands the evidence

I'm a CBT therapist and researcher based in Edinburgh. I hold an MSc in Psychological Therapies with distinction from the University of Edinburgh, specialising in adapting CBT for children and young people.


I'm actively involved in research funded by the Moray Endowment Fund, investigating brief low mood intervention success across Scottish mental health services. I believe therapy should be collaborative, transparent, and give you skills you can use for the rest of your life.
